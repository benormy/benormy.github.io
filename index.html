VIDEO GAMES

THINK IT, MAKE IT

Why video games?

You might be wondering: What is the purpose of this website, and why should I care? This website has a very simple goal: to show how video games can be a very good form of art, and what elements make a video game so great. As an added bonus, I will also talk about my favorite game of all time, and how it encompasses all of the elements of a great game.

I wish that my website shows you the wonders of video games, and how playing one can make you appreciate the time and effort put into making them.

What makes a good video game?

World

The first element that we will discuss is also the very first thing that you will see when you start a game. A game's world helps to immerse the player and get them involved in everything else the game has to offer. Open world games are specially good at creating a beautiful and diverse world to explore and get lost in. The Legend of Zelda: Breath of the Wild (see image above) is a great example. Not only is the world huge and beautiful, but it is also chock-full of interesting sites to see and an endless number of missions to complete.

Story

Having a beautiful world is great and all, but what is the point of having one if you don't have a narrative to follow and experience. That's where the story comes into play! Meeting new characters, learning more about a world's past, and the reasons why your character plays a key role in the story adds to the gaming experience. Most retro games did not focus on the story, so they only had small objectives like saving the princess or saving the world from monsters. When video games started to evolve, the story also became a crucial part of the industry. RPG's like Final Fantasy VII nail the story aspect perfectly. You keep learning about the world you are playing in and the situation it's in. It almost feels like watching a great movie!

Characters

The world has now been created and been given a narrative. There is only one issue: your character will be alone and the player will get bored instantly. That is why a game must be home to a diverse cast of characters to talk and interact with. Even the character you are playing must be interesting enough for the player to relate to. The secondary characters that the player meets must be well developped and have their own unique personality traits. EarthBound is an amazing game from the Golden Era of RPG's. Your character will make many new friend who will join him on his journey to save the world. Your party members all have their own backstories and personality traits, which makes them all the more lovable.

Music

Picture this: You are in a climactic battle with the final boss of an action-adventure game. The fight is epic on its own, but there is also something else that gives you the will to keep fighting. That, of course, is the epic music you're hearing. Music in video games have always been key part part of the experience. Whether you're exploring the world, fighting an enemy, or playing a mini-game, having the right music to fit will make the gaming experience a lot more immersive. Take the game Flower for example. The music is absolutely phenomenal. It changes depending on the setting, and it also fits every situation perfectly. Video game music is also very good to listen to on its own.

My favorite video game

The Legend of Zelda: Ocarina of Time is a masterpiece in every sense of the word. Not only those it meet all the requirements discussed earlier, but it also set a bar for future game in the action-adventure genre. Originally released on the Nintendo 64 in 1998, Ocarina of Time quickly became a must-play for all gamers. Not only was it the very first Zelda title to be made in full 3D, but it was regarded as one of, if not the best video games of all time. There are many reasons why Ocarina of Time is seen a a masterpiece, and I will explain to you why. First of all, Ocarina of Time gave you a gigantic world to explore (at the time, of course). It contained many diverse locations like expansive fields, deep forests, burning deserts, and so much more.

Secondly, the controls were very easy to learn, but also gave the player the ability to pull of some precise tricks. Thirdly, this game is home to a wide range of lovable and deep characters. Even non-important characters are filled with personality. Additionally, Ocarina of Time has an amazing story from start to finish. You will never feel like you are being dragged into the story. Instead, the game slowly teaches you about the game's mechanics and explains the story at a good pace. You will even be able to travel in time, which allows for some great story elements. Last but not least, the soundtrack is absolutely beautiful. Every track will stay embeded in your mind for the entirety of your playthrough. In conclusion, The Legend of Zelda: Ocarina of Time is an absolute joy to play, which is why it is still my favorite video game of all time.
